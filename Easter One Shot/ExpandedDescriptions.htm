Shadowdark One-Shot · 1–2 Players · Level 1 · ~4 Hours

The Hollow Warren

Expanded descriptions of the Cloverwick Dungeon
Room Reference
🌿 Area 1 — The Mouth
  • Visual Description: The entrance is a wide oval tunnel bored into a grassy hillside, framed by twisted hawthorn roots that arch overhead like a natural doorway. The dirt walls are packed smooth by generations of giant rabbits and threaded with pale root systems that hang like curtains. Wild clover grows in patches along the floor, and the light fades quickly past the threshold. The air smells of loam and something faintly sweet — like a bakery just out of sight. As players move deeper, they'll notice the first hints of wrongness: a clover patch that blooms vivid pink instead of white, a root that pulses with a soft lavender light if touched.
🔀 Area 2 — The Burrow Cross
  • Visual Description: The tunnel widens into a rough crossroads chamber, the ceiling vaulting up just enough to stand comfortably. The left passage breathes out warm lavender light that pulses slowly like a sleeping heartbeat. The right passage is dark and rich with the smell of chocolate and earth. The walls of the junction itself are covered in overlapping scratch marks — generations of rabbits marking territory — but some of the scratches have been filled in with pastel-colored clay, making them look almost like crude artwork. The abandoned egg basket sits in the center, its woven sides still bright and cheerful against the dark dirt.
🛒 Area 2b — The Warren Market
  • Visual Description: The chamber is surprisingly spacious — a natural bubble in the earth roughly thirty feet across, with a ceiling high enough that the Merchant Hares' cloth banners (scraps of colorful fabric, tied to hanging roots) can sway freely. The flat stone stalls are arranged in a loose crescent, each one draped with more fabric scraps and decorated with small objects: pebbles, acorns, dried flowers. The floor has been swept clean of loose dirt with what must have been painstaking effort, giving it an almost cobblestone appearance. Pastel light emanates from a cluster of luminous mushrooms in the far corner, casting the whole market in a warm, carnival glow. Each Merchant Hare has polished its stall's wares to a shine.
🐣 Area 3 — The Nursery
  • Visual Description: This room is the heart of the Corruption's gentler side, and it shows. The walls are a warm rose pink, smooth as plaster, and covered in swirling patterns that slowly rotate if you watch them long enough — like a very slow kaleidoscope. Dozens of earthen alcoves line the walls at varying heights, each one cradling a large rabbit egg that glows its own distinct pastel hue: mint green, butter yellow, lilac, coral. The floor is blanketed in soft dry grass, several inches thick. The two sleeping children are curled together in the largest nest at the room's center, their chests rising and falling peacefully. The air here is warmer than the rest of the warren and smells of fresh hay and lavender.
🖼️ Area 3b — The Painted Hall
  • Visual Description: Unlike the organic curves of the rest of the warren, this tunnel is almost architectural — long, straight, and narrow, roughly eight feet tall and four feet wide. Every inch of the smoothed walls is covered in murals painted in vivid pastel pigments that show no signs of age or fading. The figures in the paintings are rendered in a flat, iconic style — almost like illuminated manuscript art — with halos of gold around the idol and its worshippers. The floor here is bare packed earth, clean and cold. A single root-mounted lantern (now glowing a soft peach color rather than flame-yellow) still hangs from the ceiling at the hall's midpoint. The leather satchel dangles from its root peg near the final mural. The air is very still.
🍬 Area 4 — The Candy Vein
  • Visual Description: The Candy Vein hits the players like a wall of warmth and sweetness. The crystallized sugar seam runs diagonally across the chamber from floor to ceiling, roughly two feet wide, glowing a deep amber-gold from within like a lit stained glass window. Smaller veins branch off it like tributaries, threading into the walls in thin golden lines. The floor around the seam is slick and glittering where chunks have fallen and re-crystallized flat. The walls flanking the vein are furred with tiny sugar crystals that catch the light and scatter it into hundreds of tiny rainbow pinpricks across the chamber. The child's scarf is caught on a particularly jagged outcropping of crystal at shoulder height, fluttering slightly in an unfelt breeze.
